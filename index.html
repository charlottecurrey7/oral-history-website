What I Do

A guided oral history interview where you share your journey — from the path to pregnancy through birth, postpartum, and early motherhood — in your own words.

Interview & Recording Sessions

Generally run 60–90 minutes, though I leave space for more; every story unfolds at its own pace. All sessions are offered virtually via Zoom or in person in the New York City area. Rates available upon inquiry. Sessions may also be purchased as a gift.

Transcription, Editing, & Preservation

These sessions are gentle and conversational, designed to make you feel comfortable as you reflect and share. Each includes a professionally edited audio recording and a full transcript of your story, preserved as a keepsake for your family. Your story becomes a gift for yourself, your child, and generations to come.

About

I’m Romy March David, an oral historian and storyteller with a master’s degree in Oral History from Columbia University. I created The First Chapter to help women find and tell their birth stories, which so often fade into a blur in the tender days of early motherhood.

Before Columbia, I worked in journalism, where I fell in love with listening closely and telling stories with honesty and depth. My work has always centered on women’s health and preserving voices that often go unheard.

Through guided oral history interviews, I help mothers reflect on their journeys to pregnancy, birth, postpartum, and early motherhood — capturing and preserving these stories so they can be remembered for generations.
